LEGO

BrickLink Studio renderer

2020
Self-study

This project explored new marketing opportunities for LEGO by creating media applications aimed at adults and seniors who grew up with the brand. While LEGO is strongly associated with childhood play, it holds deep nostalgic value for many older users who remember it as a creative, hands-on experience. The goal was to reconnect with this audience and renew their engagement with the brand.

Using brand and market analysis — including LEGO’s 5P framework, SWOT, and positioning studies — the work identified a shift from purely playful, child-oriented products toward experiences that feel sophisticated, educational, and emotionally resonant for adults. Media applications were designed to communicate both creativity and quality while sparking personal memories, reframing LEGO as more than a toy: a platform for expression, learning, and nostalgia.

The resulting concept positioned LEGO as a lifelong creative companion, balancing its playful heritage with a more refined, knowledge-driven identity. Visual storytelling and media touchpoints were crafted to feel both familiar and elevated, helping the brand strengthen its equity among longtime fans while opening new opportunities in the growing adult builder market.

"To survive in new market, LEGO needs to become a platform for expression, learning, and nostalgia."
